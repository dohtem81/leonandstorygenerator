Aboard "Spark 7", the morning smelled of pancakes.

Not ordinary pancakes.

These were **end-of-year pancakes**, which according to the dragon were the most important pancakes in the entire galaxy.

They were perfectly golden.

Fluffy.

Drizzled with syrup.

And stacked in a great tower so tall it swayed slightly.

The dragon stood beside them in his chef's hat and gazed at his creation with pride.

— Perfection.

Leon sat at the table, swinging his legs.

He was excited.

Very.

Because until the end of the school year there were only…

**four days left.**

— Four! — he said for the tenth time that morning.

— I know — sighed the dragon. — You've already counted on the ceiling, on the wall, on the window and on my stomach.

Leon smiled broadly.

— Because it's almost the holidays!

The dragon sat down opposite him.

— And what is so exciting about that?

Leon looked at him as if he had just heard the greatest nonsense in the universe.

— Are you seriously asking?

— Yes.

Leon sat up straight.

— No lessons. No tests. You can sleep in. You can play. You can meet friends. You can do anything!

The dragon thought.

— Sounds like a Tuesday for a dragon.

— Dragon!

— What? We don't have school.

Leon narrowed his eyes.

— And that's exactly why you don't understand the magic.

The dragon sighed.

— I'd like to understand.

Leon looked at him craftily.

And then he had an idea.

The kind of idea after which the Storygenerator usually starts flickering nervously.

— You know what? I can show you.

The Storygenerator immediately lit up yellow.

— *Warning: Leon's idea detected.*

The dragon looked suspicious.

— What are you planning?

Leon smiled broadly.

— We're going to give you… school.

Silence fell.

The dragon blinked.

— …what?

---

An hour later the deck of "Spark 7" looked like the strangest classroom in history.

Leon had made a blackboard out of the navigation screen.

He had arranged chairs in neat rows.

He had even written in chalk:

**"GALACTIC SCHOOL — LAST 4 DAYS"**

The dragon sat at a desk.

He looked terrified.

He had a backpack.

Small.

Pink.

— Why pink? — he asked, offended.

— Because that's what I found.

— I look ridiculous.

— Exactly like every pupil at the end of term.

The dragon puffed out his cheeks.

Leon clapped his hands.

— Right, class, we're starting with maths!

The dragon groaned.

— I don't like this already.

Leon wrote on the blackboard:

**32 pancakes – 17 pancakes = ?**

The dragon looked.

Furrowed his brow.

Counted on his claws.

Counted for a long time.

A very long time.

Finally he raised his paw.

— Answer: not enough pancakes.

Leon burst out laughing.

— Wrong.

— But emotionally correct.

— That is actually true.

---

Then came language arts.

— Make a sentence with the word "holidays."

The dragon raised his paw.

— "On holidays I eat more pancakes."

— Good.

— Seriously?!

— Yes.

The dragon sat up proudly.

— I am a linguistic genius.

Then came science.

— What are the seasons?

The dragon thought.

— Pancake season… more pancake season… and syrup season?

Leon dissolved into laughter.

— That's wrong!

— It's a cultural matter.

---

At last Leon looked at the clock.

He smiled broadly.

— Right. The most important part.

The dragon swallowed.

— An exam?

Leon shook his head.

And cried out as joyfully as he could:

— **THE END-OF-YEAR BELL!**

He turned on the ship's alarm.

**DING DING DING DING**

The dragon nearly jumped out of his scales.

Leon leapt from his chair.

— FINISHED! FREEDOM! HOLIDAYS!

He ran around the deck, laughing.

Jumping.

Spinning pirouettes.

The dragon watched, dazzled.

And then…

he understood.

His eyes went wide.

— Ohhhh…

He too sprang to his feet.

— END OF SCHOOL!

He ran alongside Leon.

They waved their arms.

They laughed like mad.

The dragon accidentally let off a fart of joy.

**PRRT**

A small flame set fire to the word "mathematics."

Leon looked.

The dragon looked.

— Symbolic — said the dragon.

Leon burst out laughing.

— Very.

---

In the evening they sat together at the table eating pancakes.

The dragon looked thoughtful.

— Leon?

— Yes?

— Now I understand.

— What?

The dragon smiled broadly.

— That feeling when something hard ends and something good begins.

Leon nodded.

— Exactly.

The dragon looked at the tower of pancakes.

— That's why I made four extra ones.

— Why four?

— One for each day left until the end of term.

Leon smiled.

It was a brilliant idea.

And as they ate in silence, the Storygenerator glowed softly and wrote:

**"Countdown to holidays: started."**

Leon looked at the dragon.

The dragon looked at Leon.

And both cried out at the same time:

— ONLY FOUR DAYS LEFT!

And then they laughed so loudly that "Spark 7" gave a slight shudder.

Because sometimes the greatest adventure…

is simply knowing that the holidays are almost here. 🐉🥞🚀✨📚
