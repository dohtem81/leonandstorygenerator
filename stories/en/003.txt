Leon hesitated for only a second.

— Alright… let's keep going.

Click.

This time there was no swirling or flashing. Just… silence.

Leon stood in an ordinary room. A bit like his own, yet somehow different. On the desk lay scattered sheets of paper, crayons, and an unfinished drawing — it looked like a dragon, but without wings.

— Something isn't right here — Leon whispered.

The Storygenerator appeared beside him, but it was glowing more dimly than usual.
— Mode: someone else's world. Warning… influence limited.

— Meaning?

— You can't change everything. You can only help.

Then they heard a quiet sigh.

By the bed sat a boy. A little younger than Leon. He was staring at the sheet of paper and frowning.

— I can't — he said to himself. — This is pointless…

Leon glanced at the Storygenerator.
— Is this the story?

— Yes. And it seems to be stuck.

Leon stepped closer.
— Hey…

The boy looked up, surprised.
— You… are you real?

Leon shrugged.
— Sort of yes, sort of no. But I can see you have a problem.

The boy looked at the drawing.
— I wanted to draw a dragon. But it isn't working out. Others draw better.

Leon sat down beside him.
— And who said it has to be perfect?

— Well… because otherwise it'll look silly.

Leon was quiet for a moment. He remembered the Forest, the fear, the stumbles.

— You know what? — he said at last. — The best things I've ever done… weren't perfect at all.

The boy looked uncertain.
— Really?

— Really. Even today I almost gave up several times.

The Storygenerator beeped softly, as if to confirm.

Leon picked up a crayon and drew… enormous, slightly crooked wings on the dragon.

— Look. Maybe crooked. But it flies.

The boy smiled for the first time.
— That's true…

He picked up another crayon and drew fire. A little too big. A little too red.

But it was there.

And suddenly something changed.

The room brightened, the colours became more vivid, and the drawing… moved slightly, as if the dragon wanted to leap off the page.

The Storygenerator glowed more brightly:
— Story resumed. Block removed.

The boy looked at Leon.
— Thanks.

— For what?

— For not telling me what I was doing wrong.

Leon smiled gently.
— Sometimes all you need to do is start.

The world began to blur again.

Leon returned to his room.

The Storygenerator looked… different. As if it were bigger. More confident.

A message appeared on the screen:

— Level four: The hardest story.

Leon furrowed his brow.
— What could be harder?

The screen flickered for a moment.
