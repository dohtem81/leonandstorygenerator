Aboard "Spark 7", the atmosphere had been one of a great celebration since morning.

It was the **last day of school.**

Truly the last.

Not "three more tests and two more weeks."

Not "almost the holidays."

Just that real, wonderful, magical day, when you walk home knowing:

**the end. freedom. summer.**

Leon had barely held it together all day.

The lessons dragged on without mercy.

Even the clock seemed to slow on purpose.

During the last lesson, everyone in the class was fidgeting in their seats.

Someone was already whispering holiday plans.

Someone else was drawing ice creams in the margins of their notebook.

And when the final bell rang at last…

the whole class exploded with joy.

— HOLIDAAAAAYS!

Leon laughed along with everyone else.

High-fived.

Said his goodbyes.

It had been a really good year.

Full of learning.

Laughter.

Dance.

Adventure.

And fairly decent grades.

When he returned to the deck of "Spark 7", he was tired but happy.

— I wonder what the dragon's up to… — he murmured with a smile.

He should have known.

The moment the ship's door opened…

**SPLASH!**

An enormous water balloon burst right on his head.

Leon froze.

He stood soaked from head to toe.

Slowly wiped his face.

And heard a triumphant roar:

— AMBUSH!

In the middle of the deck stood the dragon.

In sunglasses.

With a headband.

And a gigantic water-balloon launcher.

Beside him stood huge buckets full of ammunition.

And around him…

Leon's entire class.

All of them.

Every single friend.

Laughing so hard they could barely stand.

Leon blinked.

— …what?

The dragon puffed out his chest with pride.

— I organised an end-of-year party.

Leon looked around in amazement.

— You invited everyone?!

— Yes.

— How did you know who?

The dragon shrugged.

— The Storygenerator helped. And I did a bit of eavesdropping.

The Storygenerator flickered:

— *That was only technically legal.*

Leon burst out laughing.

And then one of his friends shouted:

— Leon, you'd better duck!

Too late.

**SPLASH.**

A second balloon.

Straight in the back.

Everyone roared with laughter.

Leon looked at the dragon.

The dragon bared his teeth.

And handed him his own launcher.

— Right then. Let's begin.

Leon smiled broadly.

Oh yes.

This was war.

---

For the next two hours, "Spark 7" became total water-balloon mayhem.

Balloons flew everywhere.

Someone was hiding behind the pilot's seat.

Someone was climbing the stairs for better range.

Someone tried to negotiate a peace deal and got hit mid-sentence.

The dragon was absolutely unstoppable.

He roared a battle cry and launched three balloons at once.

Leon and his friends put together a flanking plan.

One of his friends distracted the dragon by asking:

— Hey, is it true you're scared of tiny spiders?

— WHAT?! NO—

**SPLASH SPLASH SPLASH**

Three balloons at once.

The dragon squealed:

— THAT'S A TRICK!

— WAR HAS ITS OWN RULES! — Leon shouted back.

Everyone laughed so hard their stomachs hurt.

At one point the dragon ducked behind the sofa and panted:

— Leon!

— What?!

— This is the best party ever!

Leon laughed.

— I know!

The dragon was so moved he let one rip.

**PRRT**

A small flame caught the balloon above his head.

**SPLASH**

A whole bucket tipped onto his snout.

Silence fell.

Everyone stared.

The dragon stood there, dripping.

Blinking slowly.

And then he said:

— …that was humiliating.

And everyone burst into such laughter that the ship rocked slightly.

---

By evening, everyone had gone home.

The deck looked like a water tornado had passed through it.

Burst balloon pieces everywhere.

Puddles.

Wet towels.

One pancake had stuck to the ceiling and no-one knew how.

Leon and the dragon stood in the middle of the chaos.

Leon sighed.

— Right, then. Clearing up.

The dragon sighed even louder.

— Worst final boss ever.

They cleaned for a long time.

Picked up balloon scraps.

Mopped the floor.

Peeled the pancake off the ceiling.

The dragon tried to eat it, but Leon said no.

— It tastes of dust.

— That's a new flavour.

— No.

They worked until late in the evening.

Tired.

Damp.

But smiling.

At last they sat down on the floor.

The deck gleamed again.

The dragon handed Leon a fresh pancake.

— For the end of the year.

Leon took a bite.

It was perfect.

He looked at the stars through the window.

And thought about everything that had happened this year.

About school.

About dancing.

About friends.

About laughter.

About adventures.

About the dragon.

— You know what? — he said quietly.

— What?

Leon smiled broadly.

— It was a really great year.

The dragon nodded.

— Yes.

Then he looked at him seriously.

— But the holidays will be even better.

Leon laughed.

And somewhere nearby the Storygenerator lit up with golden light.

On the screen appeared the words:

**"SCHOOL YEAR COMPLETE."**

And underneath:

**"NEW ADVENTURE: SUMMER."**

Leon and the dragon looked at each other.

And both knew one thing.

This was only the beginning. 🐉🥞💦🚀✨☀️
