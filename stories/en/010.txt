Leon closed his notebook and looked up at the ceiling.

— Alright… something lighter now — he muttered. — Maybe… space?

The Storygenerator, as if it had only been waiting for that, suddenly blazed with a bright, pulsing light.

— Adventure mode: COSMIC 🚀

Leon grinned broadly.
— Now that's what I'm talking about.

Click.

This time there was no soft transition.

There was a bang.

Light.

And suddenly —

— OH WO —!

Leon was sitting strapped into a seat… in a spaceship.

Outside the window: black space and thousands of stars.

— Okay… okay… this is new — he said, grabbing his harness.

The Storygenerator appeared as a small hologram on the dashboard.

— Welcome aboard the ship "Spark 7" — it announced. — Mission: recover a lost story from the edge of the galaxy.

Leon looked at it.
— A story again?

— Always.

A map appeared on the screen — swirling stars and one pulsing dot at the far edge.

— There is a planet where no one remembers… how stories end.

Leon furrowed his brow.
— Sounds familiar.

— But this time it's worse.

— Of course it is.

The ship shuddered slightly.

— Warning: entering the field of forgetting.

— The what?!

Suddenly the stars began to go out. One by one.

The silence grew heavy.

Leon looked at his hands.

— Hey… I'm… forgetting something.

— That's the effect of the field. — said the Storygenerator. — If you forget who you are… the story ends.

Leon clenched his fists.
— Then I guess I can't let that happen.

He started speaking aloud:

— My name is Leon.
— I have a brother.
— There was a ship… that we repaired.
— There was a dragon… that got wings…

Light gently returned.

— Good. Hold onto that. — said the Storygenerator.

The ship broke through the darkness.

And then —

A planet.

Grey. Silent. As if… unfinished.

They landed.

On the surface everything looked like sketches.

Houses without roofs.

Trees without leaves.

People who started sentences… and never finished them.

— "And then I…" — someone would say… and fall silent.

Leon swallowed.
