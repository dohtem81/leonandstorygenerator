Leon closed his notebook and looked up at the ceiling.

— Alright… something lighter now — he muttered. — Maybe… space?

The Storygenerator, as if it had only been waiting for that, suddenly blazed with a bright, pulsing light.

— Adventure mode: COSMIC 🚀

Leon grinned broadly.
— Now that's what I'm talking about.

Click.

This time there was no soft transition.

There was a bang.

Light.

And suddenly —

— OH WO —!

Leon was sitting strapped into a seat… in a spaceship.

Outside the window: black space and thousands of stars.

— Okay… okay… this is new — he said, grabbing his harness.

The Storygenerator appeared as a small hologram on the dashboard.

— Welcome aboard the ship "Spark 7" — it announced. — Mission: recover a lost story from the edge of the galaxy.

Leon looked at it.
— A story again?

— Always.

A map appeared on the screen — swirling stars and one pulsing dot at the far edge.

— There is a planet where no one remembers… how stories end.

Leon furrowed his brow.
— Sounds familiar.

— But this time it's worse.

— Of course it is.

The ship shuddered slightly.

— Warning: entering the field of forgetting.

— The what?!

Suddenly the stars began to go out. One by one.

The silence grew heavy.

Leon looked at his hands.

— Hey… I'm… forgetting something.

— That's the effect of the field. — said the Storygenerator. — If you forget who you are… the story ends.

Leon clenched his fists.
— Then I guess I can't let that happen.

He started speaking aloud:

— My name is Leon.
— I have a brother.
— There was a ship… that we repaired.
— There was a dragon… that got wings…

Light gently returned.

— Good. Hold onto that. — said the Storygenerator.

The ship broke through the darkness.

And then —

A planet.

Grey. Silent. As if… unfinished.

They landed.

On the surface everything looked like sketches.

Houses without roofs.

Trees without leaves.

People who started sentences… and never finished them.

— "And then I…" — someone would say… and fall silent.

Leon swallowed.
— This is… a little frightening.

He walked up to one of the figures.
— Hey… what happened?

— "It started when…" — they said… and fell silent.

— They really don't have endings — Leon whispered.

The Storygenerator appeared beside him.
— Not just endings. They've lost the middle too. Only the beginnings remain.

Leon nodded slowly.
— So we need to… help them finish their stories?

— Or remind them how it's done.

Leon looked around.

— Okay… let's try.

He walked up to a girl holding an empty book.

— What is it about? — he asked.

— "About a journey…" — she answered.

— Great. So what happened next?

The girl looked at him helplessly.

Leon sat down beside her.
— You know what… I didn't know either, once.

He picked up a stick and drew a line on the ground.

— Here's the beginning. That you have.

He drew the middle.
— Here's the moment when things get hard.

And at the end:
— And here… you decide what comes next.

The girl stared.

— But I don't know what the ending should be…

Leon smiled gently.
— It doesn't have to be perfect.

The girl narrowed her eyes.
— Maybe… the hero goes home?

The line on the ground lit up.

The book in her hands filled with words.

Colours began to return.

— Progress 12% — beeped the Storygenerator.

Leon stood up.
— Right then, let's keep going.

They walked from one story to the next.

He didn't invent everything for them.

He just helped them start… or finish.

And each time the world became a little more real.

Not perfect.

But whole.

When the last story was closed, the planet blazed like a star.

People began to speak.

To laugh.

To finish their sentences.

— "…and then I realised I could try again!" — someone cried.

Leon smiled broadly.
— That was good.

The Storygenerator appeared beside him, brighter than ever.

— Mission complete.

— Recovered: the meaning of stories.

Leon looked at the planet.
— You know what? Space is strange.

— And a little like home.

Leon laughed.
— Well… a little very much so.

On the way back, he looked out the ship's window.

The stars were shining brightly again.

— So where to next? — he asked.

The screen flickered.

— The galaxy is full of stories.

— And you already know what to do with them.

Leon rested his head against the seat and smiled.

— Then… let's keep flying.

Because this time the adventure was not an escape.

It was… a choice. 🚀✨
