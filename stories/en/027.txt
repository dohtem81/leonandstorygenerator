That morning aboard "Spark 7" the atmosphere was strange.

No alarms.

No exploding pancakes.

No jellyfish pretending to be breakfast.

Something far more serious was happening.

Leon needed **new shoes**.

He stood in the middle of the deck and stared gloomily at his old sneakers.

They looked terrible.

One had a hole.

The other squeaked.

And both looked as worn-out as the dragon after six hours of pancake-making.

The dragon looked at them and actually stepped back.

— Yikes.

— I know, — Leon sighed.

— Are they… alive?

— No.

The shoe squeaked.

The dragon narrowed his eyes.

— I'm not so sure.

Leon sighed heavily.

— I need new ones.

The dragon nodded.

— Right. We'll fly to Planet Shoeon.

Leon looked suspicious.

— That exists?

The Storygenerator lit up:

— *Yes. Capital of galactic footwear fashion.*

The dragon smiled triumphantly.

— See?

Click.

---

Five minutes later they were standing outside the largest shopping centre Leon had ever seen in his life.

It was enormous.

Seven floors.

Two hundred shops.

And escalators that went sideways too, because apparently someone had decided that normal was not enough.

Leon looked up.

— Oh no.

The dragon looked up in delight.

— Oh yes.

---

First shop.

Leon tried on shoes.

— Too tight.

Second shop.

— Too loose.

Third.

— Too shiny.

Fourth.

— Why are they playing music?!

The salesperson smiled proudly.

— Disco deluxe model.

The shoes started playing:

🎵 *Shoesy-shoesy-boom!* 🎵

Leon took them off immediately.

— Absolutely not.

Meanwhile the dragon had been sitting in the customer chair and looked worse and worse.

More and more tired.

More and more sleepy.

After six hours he sighed heavily.

— Leon…

— What?

— How much longer?

Leon was eyeing another shelf.

— I don't know. These are quite all right, but maybe those ones…

The dragon closed his eyes.

— I think something is happening to me.

Leon looked over.

The dragon looked pale.

Very pale.

— Are you all right?

The dragon shuddered.

— I think… I think I'm too tired…

Suddenly he sat bolt upright.

He made a strange noise:

— **blrp?**

Leon blinked.

— What?

The dragon tensed.

— Oh no.

— What "oh no"?

— Leon… I think I'm…

And then…

**PLOP.**

An egg dropped onto the floor.

Silence fell.

Leon looked at the egg.

Then at the dragon.

Then back at the egg.

The salesperson looked at the egg.

An elderly lady looked at the egg too.

The whole shop was staring.

The dragon blinked.

— …I laid an egg.

Leon opened his mouth.

Closed it.

Opened it again.

— YOU… WHAT?!

The dragon looked equally shocked.

— I DON'T KNOW!

The salesperson clapped his hands.

— Congratulations!

— THIS IS NOT THE TIME FOR CONGRATULATIONS! — Leon and the dragon shouted together.

And then:

**PLOP.**

A second egg.

Leon stared, baffled.

— Why?!

The Storygenerator lit up calmly.

— *Analysis: extreme fatigue in dragons can trigger emergency egg mode.*

The dragon stared blankly into space.

— I have a… what mode?

— *It's a natural defence mechanism.*

Leon frowned.

— How exactly does an egg defend anything?!

The Storygenerator paused to think.

— *Still under investigation.*

The dragon groaned.

— I want to go home.

**PLOP.**

Third egg.

— Oh no, it's speeding up!

---

Leon looked at the shelves of shoes.

At the dragon.

At the eggs.

At his old squeaky sneakers.

And suddenly…

a flash of inspiration.

He looked down.

At the shoes on his feet.

Then at the dragon.

He smiled.

— You know what?

— What?

Leon shrugged.

— Maybe my old ones aren't so bad after all.

The dragon blinked.

— Seriously?

— Seriously.

He looked around the shop and sighed.

— Not one pair was perfect.

Suddenly the dragon looked as if he had just been rescued from certain death.

His whole face relaxed.

And… the laying stopped.

Silence.

They looked at each other.

— Is it over? — Leon asked.

The dragon nodded with relief.

— Over.

Leon laughed.

— So I saved the galaxy from a dragon farm.

— Hero, — the dragon murmured.

---

When they got back to "Spark 7" they were exhausted.

Leon dropped into his chair.

The dragon slumped beside him.

Between them lay three eggs.

They looked at them for a moment.

At last Leon asked:

— What now?

The dragon thought about it.

Then smiled slyly.

— Omelette?

Leon looked at him in horror.

— Absolutely not.

The dragon sighed.

— Shame.

And then the Storygenerator flickered:

**"HOLIDAY LESSON OF THE DAY:"**
**"Not everything new is better."**

Leon looked at his old shoes.

They squeaked.

He smiled.

Maybe they'd last a little longer.

And the dragon?

The dragon was already asleep.

And in his sleep he muttered:

— …don't make me choose shoes ever again…

Leon laughed quietly.

Because the holidays were only just getting started.

And they were already truly strange. 🐉👟🥚🚀✨
