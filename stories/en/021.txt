Aboard "Spark 7" there was an unusual calm.

Truly unusual.

The dragon, true to his solemn oath after the sausage catastrophe, **had stuck to pancakes.**

And it had to be said: he was doing brilliantly.

In the cosmic kitchen he was just tossing a pancake so high it nearly touched the ceiling.

— Attention, a grand master at work! — he called out proudly.

**SPLAT.**

The pancake stuck to his face.

— …that was planned — he said from under the batter.

Leon burst out laughing, but quickly went back to checking his costume.

He was a little nervous.

But more excited.

Because tomorrow…

tomorrow he had a **great ballet performance.**

And he couldn't wait.

Not only because he would be performing on a huge stage in front of an audience.

Not only because he had been practising the routine for months.

But also because he was going to dance together with all the girls from his group.

And they were brilliant.

Funny.

Kind.

And they always cheered him up at rehearsals.

Leon adjusted his costume and smiled at his reflection.

— Tomorrow is going to be great.

The dragon peeled the pancake off his snout.

— Do you really want to do this?

Leon looked at him, surprised.

— Of course.

— Even though everyone will be watching?

— Yes.

— And you'll be dancing?

— Yes.

The dragon narrowed his eyes.

— You're strange.

Leon laughed.
— Thanks.

The dragon sat down beside him.

— But… you are a little nervous, aren't you?

Leon sighed.

— A little.

— Afraid of making a mistake?

Leon nodded.

— A bit of that. A bit of the audience. A bit of everything.

The dragon thought.

And then, very seriously, placed a paw on Leon's shoulder.

— In that case, we need to prepare you the dragon way.

Leon looked suspicious.
— What does that mean?

The dragon bared his teeth.

— Stage stress simulation.

Leon should have run away then.

But he didn't.

And that was a mistake.

---

Ten minutes later the entire deck of "Spark 7" looked like a completely mad theatre.

The dragon had arranged chairs as an audience.

On each one he had placed a pancake with a face drawn on it.

Some looked friendly.

Some looked threatening.

One had a monocle.

— Why does that one have a monocle? — Leon asked.

— He's an art critic.

— Of course.

The dragon stood to the side and bellowed:

— LADIES AND GENTLEMEN! HERE IS LEON, WHO WILL TRY NOT TO FALL OVER!

Leon groaned.

— Thanks for the encouragement.

— Begin!

Music flowed from the speakers.

Leon began to dance.

The first turn came out brilliantly.

The second too.

The third —

**PRRT**

The dragon farted out of nerves.

A tiny flame hit the critic with the monocle.

The pancake caught fire.

— DRAGON!

— That was supportive stress!

Leon tried to save the situation and slipped on the syrup.

He spun.

Turned three times.

And…

landed perfectly on one leg.

Silence fell.

The dragon blinked.

— …that was genius.

Leon blinked too.

Because it really was.

It looked great.

The Storygenerator lit up:

— *New move unlocked: Triple Syrup Pirouette.*

Leon straightened up slowly.

— Seriously?

— *Artistic rating: 9.8/10.*

The dragon began clapping so hard he knocked over two chairs.

— You see?! You're already ready!

Leon burst out laughing.

And suddenly he felt the nerves drain away a little.

Because after all…

if he could dance amid burning pancakes and dragon farts…

an ordinary stage would hold no fear.

---

The next day he stood backstage at the big theatre.

He could hear the audience.

He could hear the music.

He could see the girls from his group smiling at him and adjusting their costumes.

— Ready? — one of them asked.

Leon smiled broadly.

He thought about the dragon.

About the burning critic with the monocle.

About the pancakes.

And about that absurd training session.

— Yes — he said.

And he really meant it.

The curtain went up.

Leon ran out onto the stage.

The audience watched.

The lights shone brightly.

And he danced lightly, confidently, and with joy.

And when he did the new turn…

the one he'd learned by accident…

somewhere far away on the ship the dragon looked at the Storygenerator and said proudly:

— That's my move.

The Storygenerator flickered.

— *No.*

The dragon puffed up, offended.

But a moment later he fried a victory pancake anyway.

Because sometimes the best preparation for a great performance…

is a little chaos…

a little laughter…

and a dragon who believes in you. 🐉🥞✨🩰
