The first morning of the holidays aboard "Spark 7" was perfect.

No alarm clock.

No school.

No tests.

No "Leon, hand in your homework."

Leon stretched contentedly and looked out through the ship's window at the stars.

— Holidays… — he sighed happily.

The dragon was already awake.

He was bustling about the deck in his chef's hat, muttering to himself and stirring pancake batter.

— At last, time for serious matters.

Leon yawned.
— What kind?

The dragon looked at him with absolute gravity.

— Holiday breakfasts.

Leon laughed.

Then the Storygenerator lit up with blue light.

On the screen appeared the words:

**"HOLIDAY ADVENTURE NO. 1"**
**"PLANET PANCAKEA"**

The dragon dropped his ladle.

— Planet… what?!

— Pancakea — Leon read.

The dragon's pupils went wide.

— Of pancakes?!

Click.

---

A second later, "Spark 7" had turned into… a submarine.

Not an ordinary one.

A cosmic submarine.

It had huge windows, a periscope and a gleaming hull.

Leon let out a whistle.

— Ooh, that's brilliant.

The dragon was already pressing his snout against the glass.

— Where are the pancakes?!

They surfaced in an ocean.

But not an ordinary one.

The ocean around them was golden, fluffy and smelled of syrup.

And across its surface drifted thousands of enormous pancakes.

Some small.

Some as big as islands.

All perfectly golden-brown.

The dragon froze.

His lower lip began to tremble.

— No…

Leon looked puzzled.

— What?

The dragon pointed a claw.

— They… are more beautiful than mine.

A dramatic silence fell.

Leon blinked.

— Dragon, they're just pancakes.

The dragon turned slowly.

In his eyes burned determination.

— I must meet their chef.

— What?

— Whoever made these… is a master. I will learn everything from them.

Leon sighed.

— Okay, sounds normal for us.

---

They began to sail through the ocean of pancakes.

The sight was incredible.

Enormous pancakes drifted on the waves.

Some turned over lazily.

Some looked so perfect that the dragon squealed with delight.

— Look at that edge! What a texture!

— Dragon, it's slightly odd that you're marvelling at a pancake's structure.

— You don't understand art.

Leon rolled his eyes.

Suddenly he noticed something strange.

One of the pancakes moved.

On its own.

He frowned.

— Dragon…
— Shh. I'm admiring.
— That pancake just moved.

The dragon looked.

The pancake moved again.

Then a second one.

And a third.

Slowly…

they began to rise.

From beneath each one grew long, waving tentacles.

Leon went pale.

— Those aren't pancakes.

The dragon blinked.

— …what?

At that moment one of the creatures plastered itself to the glass.

It had a fluffy golden cap…

and gelatinous eyes.

The dragon squealed in the thinnest voice in dragon history.

— IT'S A JELLYFISH?!

The Storygenerator wailed:

— *WARNING. GELATINOUS PANCAKE JELLYFISH. ATTACKS EVERYTHING IN THE WATER.*

Leon leapt to the controls.

— Of course it does!

Suddenly the entire ocean came alive.

Hundreds of pancake jellyfish surged towards them.

They plastered themselves to the windows.

They pounded against the hull.

Tentacles lashed the water.

The dragon was close to fainting.

— They tricked me visually!

— This is not the time for culinary betrayal!

Leon pushed to full power.

The sub lurched forward.

But the jellyfish were fast.

Very fast.

One wrapped itself around the propeller.

Another blocked the periscope.

A third climbed onto the dragon's snout.

— AAAA GET IT OFF!

Leon wrestled with the helm.

— Get it off yourself!

— IT'S TOUCHING ME WITH JELLY!

In a panic the dragon let out an enormous fart.

**PPRRRRROOOOOT**

A jet of flame shot straight at the jellyfish.

**FLUMP**

It exploded in a shower of syrup.

Leon's eyes went wide.

— Dragon…
— What?!

Leon bared his teeth.

— That works.

The dragon blinked.

Then understood.

He looked at the oncoming jellyfish with a warrior's glare.

— Oh no.

He planted himself like a warrior-dragon.

— You want a taste of pancake fury?!

And then…

he began firing battle-farts.

**PRRT! FLUMP! PRRRT! BLUMP!**

The jellyfish exploded one by one in a rain of syrup.

Leon laughed and steered a slalom.

— Left!
— PRRT!
— Right!
— PPRT!
— Up!
— THAT REQUIRED PREPARATION!

But somehow…

they made it.

The last jellyfish exploded just behind them, and the sub surfaced safely onto a calm stretch of water.

Silence fell.

Leon was breathing hard.

So was the dragon.

Both of them were covered in syrup.

The dragon stared glumly at the ocean.

— So there was no master chef after all.

Leon patted him on the shoulder.

— Easy there.

The dragon sighed.
— My pancakes are better anyway.

Leon smiled broadly.

He looked at the stars reflected in the water.

At the calm ocean.

At the first truly mad holiday adventure.

And said with a glint in his eye:

— If this is how the holidays begin…

then they're going to be absolutely **explosive**.

The dragon blinked.

Then snorted with laughter.

— Was that a joke about exploding jellyfish?

Leon shrugged.

— Maybe.

The dragon nodded with respect.

— Good one.

And somewhere in the background the Storygenerator flickered:

**"HOLIDAYS: STARTED."**

And underneath it added:

**"And that's only day one."** 🐉🥞🚀🌊✨💥
