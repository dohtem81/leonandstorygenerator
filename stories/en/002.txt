Leon barely had time to blink before the world melted again like ice cream in the sun.

— Adventure mode: level two! — announced the Storygenerator, spinning in the air a little faster than before. — This time… something is wrong with the stories.

Leon looked around.

They were standing in the middle of a city that looked familiar, yet was strangely… empty. The houses were grey, the trees leafless, and the people walked slowly, as if they had forgotten where they were going.

— What happened here? — Leon asked, more quietly than usual.

— Someone… stole the endings — replied the Storygenerator. — Without endings, stories lose their meaning. And without stories… the world begins to fade.

Leon furrowed his brow.
— Who could do something like that?

At that moment a cold wind blew, and a shadow appeared on the wall of one of the buildings. It had no face, only an outline that rippled like smoke.

— They call me the Collector — said the shadow. — I collect endings. The best moments. Why should they go to waste?

Leon felt a shiver.
— But then no one knows what happens next…

— Exactly — replied the shadow. — And so everything stops at the most interesting point.

The Storygenerator beeped a warning:
— Note: the opponent likes cliffhangers.

Leon sighed.
— Great…

— If you want to get the endings back — the Collector continued — you must create new ones. Better ones. Ones that I cannot take away.

— And how am I supposed to do that? — Leon asked.

The shadow seemed to smile.
— That is your problem.

And it vanished.

On the ground appeared three glowing doors.

On the first, a sign read:
"A Story Without Fear"

On the second:
"A Story Without Failure"

On the third:
"A True Story"

The Storygenerator hovered next to Leon.
— The choice matters. Each door is a different path… and a different ending.

Leon looked for a moment.

— Without fear sounds easy…
— Without failure… even easier…
— But a true one?

He clenched his fists.

— True stories are the best — he said at last. — Even if they are difficult.

And he opened the third door.

He immediately found himself in a dark forest. He could hear his own heartbeat. Every step was uncertain. Along the way he stumbled several times, and once he even wanted to turn back.

— Maybe this was the wrong choice… — he muttered.

— That is part of the story — replied the Storygenerator, calmer than usual.

At last Leon reached a small light.

There, in a clearing, lay a chest. Inside — hundreds of endings. Happy ones. Sad ones. Brave ones. Quiet ones.

Leon took one.

But instead of keeping it… he opened it.

Light poured out all around.

The forest disappeared.

The city came back to life. People began to laugh, the trees turned green, and the colours returned as after a storm.

The Collector appeared once more, but this time it was smaller.

— What have you done? — it asked.

— I didn't keep the ending — replied Leon. — I shared it.

The shadow trembled… and dissolved into the air.

The Storygenerator beeped cheerfully:
— New skill unlocked: Creating Endings.

Leon returned to his room.
