Aboard "Spark 7," there had been controlled chaos since morning.

That is to say…

Leon called it "preparations."

The dragon called it "a culinary symphony of genius."

The Storygenerator called it:

**"Fire hazard level: moderate."**

It was the perfect holiday evening for **a cookout with friends**.

Leon had invited his friends.

The dragon had taken his role as co-host very seriously and had been stalking around since dawn in a huge apron reading:

**"HEAD OF THE PAN"**

He looked absurdly proud.

On one side of the deck Leon stood at the large cosmic grill.

Steaks sizzled on the rack.

Perfect.

Juicy.

Smelling so good that even the Storygenerator purred with approval.

After the hellfire sausage disaster, Leon had taken full control of the meat.

The dragon had got his safe zone.

The pan.

The batter.

Pancakes.

And it had to be said:

he made them brilliantly.

He tossed them with such grace, as if performing ballet.

— See? — he said proudly. — I've found my calling.

Leon flipped a steak.

— Yes, and the galaxy is safer for it.

The dragon narrowed his eyes.

— Was that meant to be a compliment?

— Yes.

— You delivered it oddly.

---

The guests began to arrive.

The deck quickly filled with laughter.

Music.

Conversation.

Someone was playing cards.

Someone was watching the stars through the panoramic window.

Someone was already stealing pancakes.

The dragon caught one of Leon's friends red-handed.

— Hey!

The boy froze with a pancake in his mouth.

The dragon narrowed his eyes.

— Well?

The boy chewed slowly.

His eyes went wide.

— That's the best pancake I've ever eaten.

The dragon straightened up so proudly that his back cracked.

— I knew it.

---

At the grill, Leon was serving steaks.

— Medium done, — he announced with a professional air.

Several people looked delighted.

But one of his friends wrinkled her nose.

— Do you have well done?

Silence fell.

Leon looked at her calmly.

The dragon looked at her with culinary horror.

Even the Storygenerator dimmed slightly.

— Well done? — Leon repeated.

— You know… more cooked through?

The dragon clutched his chest.

— Leon… is she insulting the meat?

Leon sighed theatrically.

— No harm done. Everyone strays at some point.

His friend narrowed her eyes.

— Hey!

Leon smiled.

— Give me a moment.

He put one steak back on the grill.

Cooked it longer.

And longer.

And longer still.

The dragon turned away.

— I can't watch.

The Storygenerator displayed:

**"A moment of silence for the juiciness."**

At last Leon handed over the steak.

His friend bit in.

She chewed.

For a long time.

A very long time.

Then she tried to take a drink.

Then chewed again.

And looked at Leon.

— Okay.

Leon raised an eyebrow.

— Okay?

She sighed heavily.

— Medium done is better.

The dragon leapt up.

— I KNEW IT!

Several people started clapping.

Someone shouted:

— Leon has saved another one!

Leon gave a small bow.

— It's a heavy mission, but someone has to do it.

---

The evening kept getting better.

The dragon served pancakes.

Leon tended the steaks.

The guests laughed, chatted and watched the stars.

Someone put on music.

A few people started dancing.

One of the girls looked at Leon.

— Show us that ballet spin of yours.

Leon smiled slightly.

— Seriously?

— Seriously.

The dragon immediately hollered:

— THAT'S MY MOVE!

— It's not yours.

— A little bit mine.

Leon sighed and stood in the middle of the deck.

He took a breath.

He spun.

Perfect.

Light.

Fluid.

Everyone began to clap.

The dragon was so moved he let out a little toot.

**prrt**

A tiny flame caught the corner of his apron.

— OH NO!

In one motion Leon grabbed a bowl of pancake batter and sloshed it.

**SPLAT.**

The fire went out.

The dragon stood completely covered in batter.

He blinked.

Slowly licked some off his nose.

— …tasty.

The whole deck erupted with laughter.

---

Late that evening, when the guests had all gone home, Leon and the dragon sat on the roof of "Spark 7."

Each with a plate of the last pancakes.

They watched the stars.

And the quiet night.

— That was a good cookout, — said Leon.

The dragon nodded.

— Very good.

They were quiet for a moment.

Then the dragon looked at him.

— Holidays are wonderful.

Leon smiled broadly.

— And this is only the beginning.

The Storygenerator lit up softly and wrote:

**"HOLIDAYS CONTINUE."**

And below:

**"Happiness level: medium done."**

Leon snorted with laughter.

The dragon frowned.

— That's a medium joke at best.

— Maybe a bit.

— But the steaks were excellent.

— Thanks.

The dragon looked at his pancake.

— Mine too.

— True.

And so they sat together, eating and laughing under the stars, knowing one thing:

these holidays were only just getting started. 🐉🥞🥩🚀✨☀️
