The next day Leon didn't wait for any sign.

He didn't look at the Storygenerator in the morning.

He didn't check whether the screen was glowing.

He simply… went out.

But something was different.

As if the world were full of small, invisible "click" buttons.

At school everything started perfectly ordinarily.

Lesson. Boredom. Sheets of paper. Pen.

And then the teacher asked a question.

Silence.

Nobody raised their hand.

Leon knew the answer.

But his hand… didn't want to go up.

— A true story — it flashed through his mind.

He sighed quietly.

And raised his hand.

— Yes, Leon? — the teacher asked.

He gave the answer. A little uncertainly. A little too fast.

A brief silence.

— Good — the teacher nodded.

And that was all.

But for Leon it wasn't "all."

It was another door he had opened by himself.

At break he noticed something else.

The same boy as yesterday.

But this time… he wasn't sitting alone.

He was talking to someone.

And every now and then… he was smiling.

Leon stopped for a second.

— Well, look at that…

He didn't go over.

He didn't need to.

The story was already moving forward.

In the afternoon he came home and — more out of curiosity than need — looked at the Storygenerator.

The screen was dark.

Leon sat down.

— You know what… I think I'm starting to understand.

Silence.

— You haven't disappeared.

He touched the casing gently.

— You just… stopped speaking first.

The screen flickered.

For a brief moment a single sentence appeared:

— Exactly.

Leon smiled broadly.

— Clever.

He took his notebook.

Not because he had to.

Only because he wanted to.

On a new page he wrote:

"Stories That Really Happened"

And he began:

— "Today someone raised their hand, even though they hesitated."

— "Today someone sat down next to someone who usually sits alone."

— "Today someone tried again."

He stopped.

— And what next?

This time he didn't wait for an answer.

He added:

— "Tomorrow… we'll see."

He closed the notebook.

Outside the window it was beginning to get dark.

The world looked quiet.

But Leon already knew something he hadn't known before:

The greatest stories don't have great special effects.

They have… a continuation.

And he was right in the middle of one. ✨
