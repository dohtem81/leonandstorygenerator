In the evening the ship was strangely quiet.

The dragon slept curled up against the wall, mumbling in his sleep:
— …don't burn the sausage… it's an art…

Leon sat by the window and looked at the stars.

The Storygenerator lit up gently.

— *New story: The Boy Who Didn't Want to Dance.*

Leon smiled slightly.
— Ooh… so there'll be stress.

— *A lot of stress.*

Click.

---

Leon found himself backstage in a great hall.

Chaos reigned everywhere.

Someone was fixing the decorations.

Someone was running with a hat.

Someone was shouting:
— Where is the right-side chicken from scene three?!

— …I don't want to know — Leon muttered.

In the corner sat a boy in a strange stage costume. He looked as if he wanted to disappear.

He was squeezing his hands so tightly that his knuckles had gone white.

— Is that him? — Leon asked.

— *Yes.*

Leon sat down beside him.
— Hey.

The boy jumped.
— Aah! Who are you?!

— Long story. Short version: sometimes I help in odd moments.

The boy groaned.
— You've come to the wrong place then. This is the worst moment of my life.

Leon looked at the stage.
— That bad?

— I have to go out and dance in front of THE WHOLE SCHOOL!

— Okay, that is intense.

— And my parents are sitting in the front row! — the boy looked as if he were about to evaporate.

Leon nodded with understanding.
— So your brain is telling you: "run into the forest and live with the squirrels."

The boy stared at him.
— …exactly that.

— Classic panic mode.

---

From behind the curtain came applause.

The boy turned even paler.
— I can't do this.

— You can.

— I can't!

— You can be scared and still do it.

The boy furrowed his brow.
— That makes no sense.

— That's roughly how courage works — Leon shrugged. — It's not the absence of fear. It's moving despite it.

The boy looked at his shoes.
— And if I make a mistake?

Leon smiled gently.
— Then I have important news for you.

He leaned in conspiratorially.

— Almost everyone on that stage is scared too.

— Really?

— Even the ones who look confident.

The boy peeked from behind the curtain.
— That boy too?

— One hundred percent.

— And that girl?

— Probably even more so.

— And the teacher?

Leon thought for a moment.
— Oh, she's definitely faking the professionalism.

The boy burst out laughing.

And his shoulders relaxed a little.

---

Suddenly —

— THIRTY SECONDS! — someone cried from backstage.

The boy froze.
— Oh no oh no oh no…

Leon quickly stood up.
— Alright. Emergency plan.

— What?!

— Don't try to dance perfectly.

— WHAT?!

— Try to dance… all the way to the end.

The boy stared for a moment.
— Just that?

— Just that.

The Storygenerator flickered:
— *Good advice detected.*

— Thanks — Leon muttered.

---

The music began to play.

The curtain went up.

The boy took a step.

Then a second.

His hands were trembling slightly.

The first turn came out crooked.

He looked at Leon in panic.

Leon gave him a thumbs up.

— Keep going!

The boy kept dancing.

And suddenly…

he stopped thinking about everyone watching.

He focused on the music.

On the movement.

On simply being in the middle of that moment.

And then he really started to dance.

Not perfectly.

But truly.

---

When the music stopped, for one second there was silence.

That terrible second when you think:
"oh no…"

And then —

APPLAUSE!

Enormous.

Loud.

The boy blinked in disbelief.

In the audience his parents were clapping loudest of all.

The boy looked at Leon from behind the stage.
— I… did it.

Leon smiled broadly.
— Yes.

— And I didn't even cry!

— That's also a success.

---

Backstage the boy was breathing fast, but already smiling.

— I'm still shaking a little.

— That's normal after brave things.

The Storygenerator glowed warmly:
— *Mission complete: fear didn't stop the story.*

The boy looked at the stage.
— You know what… I think I'd like to dance again sometime.

Leon laughed.
— Ooh, that's dangerous. That's how passions start.

---

Back on the ship, Leon sank heavily into his seat.

— Phew. I was more stressed than he was.

The dragon opened one eye.
— Were there pancakes?

— Not everything has to have pancakes.

The dragon closed his eye.
— Sad.

Leon looked at the Storygenerator.
— But seriously… courage is strange.

— *Why?*

— Because people think they need to stop being scared first… and only then do something.

— *But it works the other way round.*

Leon smiled gently.
— Yes. A little unfair.

Outside the window the stars moved slowly past.

And somewhere far away…

some boy was lying in bed thinking:

"That was terrifying."

…and at the same time:

"I want to do it again." ✨
