The next day Leon woke up with a strange feeling.

As if something had changed… but he couldn't quite say what.

The Storygenerator stood on the desk. Quiet. Ordinary.

No lights.

— Okay… so it wasn't a dream — he muttered, stretching.

He went downstairs, had breakfast, and everything looked normal.

Until school.

In the hallway he noticed a boy he recognised by sight. He always sat alone. Always with his head down.

Today too.

Leon slowed his pace.

— You are the Storygenerator.

That sentence came back to him suddenly.

— Seriously? — he whispered to himself. — I just… go up to him?

He hesitated.

This wasn't a dragon.

There was no mission.

There was no "click."

There was only a decision.

He sighed and walked over.

— Hey.

The boy looked up, surprised.
— What?

Leon wasn't sure what to say. No ready-made script.

— Nice hoodie — he said at last.

The boy looked at it as if seeing it for the first time.
— Uh… thanks.

A brief silence.

Leon was about to walk away…

— Nobody's ever said it was nice before — the boy added quietly.

Leon smiled slightly.
— Well, someone had to start.

And he walked on.

That was all.

Small. Brief.

But something in the air… had shifted.

In the afternoon Leon came home.

He threw down his bag, looked at the Storygenerator.

— Was that it?

The device didn't answer.

But the screen… flickered.

For a moment.

And a short message appeared:

— Recorded.

— Small story started.

Leon sat on the bed.
— Seriously? That's enough?

The screen changed once more:

— That's always how it begins.

Leon nodded slowly.

— That's a little terrifying.

— And a little good.

Leon smiled to himself.
— Well… a little very much so.
