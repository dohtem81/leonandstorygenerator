The "Spark 7" glided silently among the stars. Leon sat in the pilot's seat and watched the lights slowly sliding past the window.

— Alright… where to now? — he asked, partly curious, partly excited.

The Storygenerator appeared as a hologram, but this time… it didn't look the way it usually did.

It flickered.

— Hey… everything okay? — Leon furrowed his brow.

— Interference… detected… — the voice was cutting in and out. — New sector… unstable…

— Meaning? — Leon leaned forward.

— Stories… are mixing.

— That sounds like a problem.

— That sounds like chaos.

Suddenly the map exploded with colours.

Hundreds of points began overlapping, colliding, disappearing and reappearing.

— Okay, that looks bad — said Leon.

— Emergency landing recommended.

— Sure, why not — he sighed. — Let's go.

The landing wasn't hard, but it was enough for Leon to grab his seat.

— I'm alive… — he muttered.

The ship's door opened with a hiss.

Outside…

everything was mixed up.

On one side: a desert.

On the other: snow.

Above him flew… fish (again!), but between them fragments of buildings were drifting past.

— Okay… someone really went overboard here — said Leon.

The Storygenerator appeared, but its image was "breaking up."

— Many stories… have collided… in one place.

Leon took a few steps.

Suddenly a knight appeared before him.

— I must rescue the princess! — he cried.

Before Leon could say anything, an astronaut appeared beside him.

— No! First the ship must be repaired!

And behind them a dragon leapt out.

— And I want pancakes!

Leon blinked.
— Okay… I've seen this before, but not quite like this…

— The characters… don't know which story they belong to — the Storygenerator explained.

— So everyone is pulling in a different direction?

— Exactly.

Leon sighed.
— Alright… we need to sort this out somehow.

He walked up to the knight.
— Hey. Where are you?

— In my story! — he replied proudly.

— And where does it take place?

The knight looked around… desert, snow, space.
— …um…

Leon nodded.
— Exactly.

Then to the astronaut:
— You?

— On a space mission!

— And where is your ship?

The astronaut looked at a piece of a castle floating in the air.
— …that's probably not it.

Leon smiled gently.
— So you're all a little… out of place.

He gathered them together.

— Listen. Every story has its own world.

He drew three circles on the ground.

— Here's the knight — castle, dragon, princess.

— Here's the astronaut — ship, stars, mission.

— And here… — he looked at the dragon — pancakes.

— YES! — the dragon cried.

Leon laughed.
— Alright, pancakes have their own story too.

— Good approach — beeped the Storygenerator.

Leon looked at all of them.
— The problem is that everything got mixed up. So everyone has to go back… to their own story.

— But how? — the knight asked.

Leon thought for a moment.

— Start with what is yours.

— I… rescue the princess — said the knight.

The world around him began to change. The desert turned into green hills.

— I repair the ship — said the astronaut.

The stars returned, and pieces of metal began to fit together.

— I MAKE PANCAKES! — the dragon cried.

And… the smell became really good.

Leon stood in the middle, watching the chaos separate.

The colours settled.

The worlds returned to their places.

— Stabilising: 68%… 82%… 100%.

Suddenly everything fell silent.

Only one world remained.

Coherent.

Calm.

The Storygenerator stopped flickering.

— Well done.

Leon stretched.
— You know what? That was more organising than adventure.

— Sometimes that's the same thing.

Leon smiled slightly.
— I think I'm starting to get it.

They returned to the ship.

Before the doors closed, Leon looked at the world one more time.

Every story was in its place.

Not perfect.

But… fitting.

— Ready for the next one? — he asked.

The Storygenerator glowed steadily.

— Always.

Leon turned on the engines.

And this time he didn't wonder what would happen next.

Because he knew one thing:

No matter if it's chaos, silence, or space…

he could already find a story in it. 🚀✨
