Leon was sitting on the deck of "Spark 7", right next to the **on-board cosmic grill** that the Storygenerator had once installed after the dragon accidentally set fire to the kitchen while trying to make "quick pancakes."

Ever since, a rule had been in place:

**"The dragon only grills under supervision."**

The dragon stood proudly at the grill in an enormous chef's hat.

— Today, Leon, you will try my masterpiece.

Leon looked suspicious.

— Last time your "masterpiece" melted half the cutlery.

— That was an experimental texture.

Leon sighed.
— Of course.

A sausage was sizzling on the grate.

It looked perfect.

It smelled great.

The dragon was practically glowing with pride.

— I bought it myself — he said. — I read the labels.

Leon raised an eyebrow.
— Really?

— Almost all the words.

Leon should have suspected something then.

But he didn't.

He took the first bite.

And immediately stiffened.

His eyes went wide.

Very wide.

— Leon? — the dragon asked.

Leon opened his mouth.

— HHHHHHHHHHHHAAAAAAAAAAAA.

It wasn't a scream.

It was the sound of a person whose tongue had just made contact with the surface of the sun.

— WATER!

He flung himself at the ship's fridge.

He drank the juice.

The milk.

A yoghurt.

Two ice cubes.

Half a stick of butter.

Nothing helped.

— I CAN FEEL MY TONGUE DETACHING FROM REALITY!

The dragon turned pale.

— Impossible… I bought an ordinary sausage!

Leon grabbed the packaging.

He looked.

And froze.

He read slowly:

**"Cosmic Hellfire Sausage MAXIMUM INFERNO — heat level: banned in 14 galaxies."**

Silence fell.

Leon looked at the dragon.

The dragon looked at the label.

— Oh.

— "Oh"?! — Leon rasped.

— I thought "inferno" was a brand.

Leon stared at him for so long that the dragon started to shrink.

— And the skull in flames?!

— I thought it was a premium logo.

Leon sank into his seat.

— Dragon…

— Yes?

— You make great pancakes.

The dragon blinked.

— Thanks?

— Maybe you should stick to those.

The dragon thought.

He looked at the grill.

He looked at the pancakes cooling nearby.

He looked at Leon, who was still breathing like a dragon after a marathon.

Finally he sighed.

— I suppose you're right.

He straightened up and said solemnly:

— I promise that next time I will be more careful about what I buy.

Leon nodded.

— Good.

At that moment the dragon let out a small fart.

**prrt**

A tiny flame shot straight under the grill.

The grill beeped:

**"ALARM. EXCESS FIRE."**

Fire suppressors fired from the ceiling.

A second later the entire deck was flooded with ice-cold foam.

Leon sat there dripping with foam.

So did the dragon.

Both were silent.

Finally Leon sighed.

— You know what?

— Yes?

— Stick to the pancakes after all.

The dragon nodded very seriously.

— It will be safer for the galaxy that way.

And this time Leon agreed with that one hundred percent. 🐉🥞🚀✨
