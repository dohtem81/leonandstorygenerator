Leon hadn't even had time to put down his plate after the last pancake when the Storygenerator blazed up like a Christmas tree.

— ADVENTURE MODE: CAMPFIRE-DRAGON 🔥🐉🥞

— Hmm… that sounds like something that could go very well or very badly — Leon muttered. — Or both.

Click.

— CRACK!

Leon landed… in a clearing.

There was a campfire.

There were sticks.

There was a sausage.

— Okay… this is suspiciously perfect — he said, sitting down by the fire.

Suddenly the familiar dragon burst out of the bushes.

— LEON! — he roared. — DISASTER!

— Again? — Leon didn't even flinch. — What is it now? Did the pancakes explode?

— WORSE!

The dragon pointed dramatically with his tail.

On the fire lay a sausage.

Black. Charred. Miserable.

— Okay… someone went a bit far with the "crispiness" — Leon assessed.

— It wasn't me! — the dragon was offended. — I just… helped a little with fire.

— "A little," meaning?

The dragon blew gently.

FWOOSH

The campfire shot up like a rocket.

Leon stepped back.
— Right. I understand the problem.

The Storygenerator appeared beside him:
— Detected: excess of fiery enthusiasm.

— You don't say.

Leon picked up a stick with a new sausage.

— Listen. A campfire isn't a dragon versus sausage.

— Then what is it?

— Teamwork.

The dragon narrowed his eyes.
— Sounds boring.

— Wait until you try it.

Leon held the sausage over the fire.

— See? Not too close. Not too far.

The dragon leaned in.
— Can I blow?

— NO.

— What about now?

— Still no.

— What about now?

— …maybe a tiny bit.

The dragon took a deep breath.

Leon squinted.
— Remember. Ti-ny.

— Okay.

The dragon blew…

…and suddenly the flame began to move gently, evenly.

The sausage began to sizzle.

— Oh — Leon smiled. — That's it.

The dragon watched as if hypnotised.
