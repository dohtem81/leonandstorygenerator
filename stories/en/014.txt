Leon hadn't even had time to put down his plate after the last pancake when the Storygenerator blazed up like a Christmas tree.

— ADVENTURE MODE: CAMPFIRE-DRAGON 🔥🐉🥞

— Hmm… that sounds like something that could go very well or very badly — Leon muttered. — Or both.

Click.

— CRACK!

Leon landed… in a clearing.

There was a campfire.

There were sticks.

There was a sausage.

— Okay… this is suspiciously perfect — he said, sitting down by the fire.

Suddenly the familiar dragon burst out of the bushes.

— LEON! — he roared. — DISASTER!

— Again? — Leon didn't even flinch. — What is it now? Did the pancakes explode?

— WORSE!

The dragon pointed dramatically with his tail.

On the fire lay a sausage.

Black. Charred. Miserable.

— Okay… someone went a bit far with the "crispiness" — Leon assessed.

— It wasn't me! — the dragon was offended. — I just… helped a little with fire.

— "A little," meaning?

The dragon blew gently.

FWOOSH

The campfire shot up like a rocket.

Leon stepped back.
— Right. I understand the problem.

The Storygenerator appeared beside him:
— Detected: excess of fiery enthusiasm.

— You don't say.

Leon picked up a stick with a new sausage.

— Listen. A campfire isn't a dragon versus sausage.

— Then what is it?

— Teamwork.

The dragon narrowed his eyes.
— Sounds boring.

— Wait until you try it.

Leon held the sausage over the fire.

— See? Not too close. Not too far.

The dragon leaned in.
— Can I blow?

— NO.

— What about now?

— Still no.

— What about now?

— …maybe a tiny bit.

The dragon took a deep breath.

Leon squinted.
— Remember. Ti-ny.

— Okay.

The dragon blew…

…and suddenly the flame began to move gently, evenly.

The sausage began to sizzle.

— Oh — Leon smiled. — That's it.

The dragon watched as if hypnotised.

— That is… beautiful.

— Easy there, it's just a sausage.

— NO. IT'S ART.

Suddenly something… dripped.

— What was that? — Leon asked.

He looked up.

In the branches of the tree… hung pancakes.

— …of course they do — he sighed.

— Those are my supplies! — the dragon said proudly. — Campfire pancakes!

One pancake fell straight onto the sausage.

SPLAT.

Silence fell.

Leon stared.

The dragon stared.

The sausage was… wrapped in a pancake.

— …what have you done — said Leon slowly.

The dragon swallowed.
— An invention?

Leon carefully removed it from the fire.

He smelled it.

— This shouldn't work.

He took a bite.

He froze.

The dragon tensed.
— Well?!

Leon looked at him slowly.
— …this is genius.

— I KNEW IT! — the dragon leapt up so enthusiastically he nearly knocked over the campfire.

The Storygenerator blazed:
— New dish unlocked: Sausage-Pancake.

— That sounds illegal — said Leon.

— That sounds delicious — the dragon corrected.

After a while they were sitting by the fire.

Eating.

Laughing.

One sausage fell into the fire.

One was eaten by the dragon… along with the stick.

— Hey! That was my stick! — Leon protested.

— It was part of the experience — the dragon replied seriously.

— Right.

As the campfire began to die down, Leon leaned back comfortably.

— You know what? That was one of the stranger adventures.

— The best! — said the dragon with his mouth full.

The Storygenerator flickered softly:
— Discovery: the best things come about by accident… and a little fire.

Leon smiled.
— And a few silly ideas.

The dragon raised his paw.
— Oh, I'm an expert at that.

Leon stood up and looked at the ship.

— Right… so what's next?

The dragon looked at him suspiciously.
— Are there more food planets?

The Storygenerator flashed:
— Always.
