Leon was sitting comfortably in the pilot's seat of "Spark 7", humming the melody from yesterday's ballet performance.

He was in a great mood.

The show had gone perfectly.

The audience had applauded.

The girls from his group had laughed and congratulated him after coming off stage.

And the dragon had spent half the evening telling everyone that **"it was actually he who choreographed the most important turn"**, even though the Storygenerator very firmly denied it.

Now, however, a new adventure awaited them.

The Storygenerator bathed the cockpit in a strange… reversed light.

Literally.

Instead of spreading outward, the light seemed to fall inward into the lamps.

— Hmm — Leon muttered. — That looks suspicious.

A message appeared on the screen:

**"PLANET UPSIDE-DOWN"**
**"Warning: everything is the wrong way round."**

The dragon swallowed.

— Everything?

— They're probably exaggerating — said Leon.

Click.

---

They weren't exaggerating.

Things started going wrong during the landing itself.

Because instead of descending to the ground…

the ship flew up even higher.

— Why are we going up?! — Leon cried.

The Storygenerator beeped:

— *On this planet "down" works the other way round.*

— Wonderful!

The dragon panicked and pressed a random button.

That actually helped.

The ship slammed with a bang into the ground.

Upside down.

Leon was hanging from his seat straps.

— Are we… standing on the ceiling?

— Apparently so — said the dragon, dangling beside him.

— I hate this planet.

---

When they went outside, it was even stranger.

Trees grew with their roots pointing at the sky.

Rain fell from the ground up into the clouds.

People walked backwards.

Birds barked.

Dogs meowed.

And in the market square someone was selling… sparklers in a fridge.

The dragon stared, dazed.

— This makes no sense.

— That's exactly the point — sighed Leon.

Suddenly a resident of the planet ran up to them.

Or rather… ran backwards away from them towards them.

— Help! — they cried. — Nobody can cope!

— What happened? — Leon asked.

— Everything is even more the wrong way round than usual!

— Meaning?

— This morning people started speaking from the ends of their sentences!

— …what?

The resident nodded.

— Bad very things are!

The dragon squeaked.
— Oh no, even the grammar is dying.

---

They were led to the palace.

The king of the planet was sitting on the ceiling and looked desperate.

— Heroes! You must help! Our Great Balance Machine has broken down!

Leon looked at the gigantic device.

It was full of levers.

But all of them had their labels the wrong way round.

"ON" meant "off."

"LEFT" meant "right."

"DO NOT TOUCH" apparently meant "definitely touch," because the dragon was already pressing it.

— DRAGON, NO!

Too late.

The machine howled.

Suddenly all the residents grew… moustaches.

Even the children.

Even the statues.

Leon stared, dumbfounded.

The dragon pulled his paw back.
— Oops.

The king stroked his new moustache.
— That's the third time this week.

Leon put his head in his hands.

— Right. I need to think.

He walked in circles.

He looked at everything.

At the reversed signs.

At the people walking backwards.

At the rain falling upwards.

At the dragon trying to eat a pancake from underneath.

And suddenly…

he stopped.

— I've got it.

Everyone looked.

— If everything here works the other way round…

The king blinked.

— Yes?

Leon smiled broadly.

— Then to fix the machine…

we need to break it even more.

Silence fell.

The dragon opened his mouth.

— That's the stupidest plan I've ever heard.

Leon nodded.

— I know. That's why it will work.

The Storygenerator flickered.

— *Logical analysis… unfortunately correct.*

---

Leon walked up to the panel.

And pressed everything wrong.

Literally everything.

He twisted "do not touch."

He switched off "on."

He pushed right when he should have pushed left.

The machine squeaked.

Sparked.

Howled.

And…

**BUMPF!**

Silence fell.

The moustaches disappeared.

The rain fell normally.

The dogs barked again.

The birds chirped.

The people turned to face forward.

The king looked in the mirror and burst into tears of joy.

— My face is back!

The whole planet erupted in celebration.

— WE DID IT!

The dragon stared with his mouth open.

— You actually worked that out.

Leon shrugged.
— Sometimes you have to think the other way round.

---

That evening a great festival was held.

Lanterns lit up the square.

Tables groaned under the weight of pancakes.

And the residents…

sang.

Literally sang about Leon.

A whole choir.

🎵
**Leon brave and Leon bold,**
**fixed our world, or so we're told!**
**Though everything was back to front,**
**he knew just what to do — well done!**
🎵

Leon went red.

— This is a little embarrassing.

The dragon wiped a tear of emotion.

— That is the most beautiful song I have ever heard.

— They're singing that I'm "as clever as a pancake."

— That is a great compliment.

The king stepped forward and presented Leon with a golden medal.

It was shaped like an upside-down pancake.

— From this day forth you are the Hero of Upside-Down!

Leon smiled broadly.

— Thanks.

The dragon nudged him with his elbow.

— See? First ballet, now hero of a planet. You're getting famous.

Leon laughed.

— And you?

The dragon straightened up proudly.

— I am the dragon who almost broke everything, but looked great doing it.

— That is actually true.

And as "Spark 7" flew off into the night sky, the song could still be heard from the planet below:

🎵
**Leon! Leon! Our hero true!**
**When things go backwards, he knows what to do!**
🎵

And Leon thought that it was quite nice…

being the hero of a planet that does everything the wrong way round. 🚀🐉🥞✨🎶
