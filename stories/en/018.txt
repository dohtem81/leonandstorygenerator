Leon and the dragon sat on the deck of "Spark 7."

Things were suspiciously quiet.

Way too quiet.

The dragon was eating pancakes. Slowly. Politely. Without sneaking from the supplies. Without crunching through the plate.

Leon stared at him with growing suspicion.

— Alright. What are you plotting?

The dragon swallowed and looked innocent.
— Me? Nothing.

— That's exactly what worries me.

The Storygenerator suddenly bathed the cockpit in red.

— *ALARM.*

Leon sighed.
— There we go.

A message appeared on the screen:

**"PLANET PANCAKEON VII"**
**"Royal crisis."**

The dragon choked on a piece of pancake.
— Oh no.

Leon narrowed his eyes.
— What "oh no"?

The dragon began nervously swinging his tail.
— Um… a small matter.

— Dragon.

— A really small matter.

— DRAGON.

— …I'm a prince there.

Silence fell.

Leon blinked.
— You're a WHAT?!

— Prince of Pancakeon. Formally the Third Dragon, Heir of the Pan, and Guardian of the Golden Sizzle.

Leon stared at him for a long time.

A very long time.

— You are a… PANCAKE ROYAL?!

The dragon lowered his head.
— It sounds silly when someone says it out loud.

Storygenerator:
— *Sounds very silly.*

— Thanks — the dragon muttered.

---

A few minutes later they landed.

The planet looked like a gigantic kitchen.

Everywhere were golden towers resembling stacks of pancakes.

Fountains poured maple syrup.

The guards had frying pans instead of shields.

Leon actually whistled.
— Okay… this is absurdly beautiful.

The palace doors flew open with a bang.

Out walked a great dragon in a crown.

Enormous.

Dignified.

He smelled of vanilla.

— MY SON! — he roared.

The little dragon beside Leon shrank.
— Oh no.

The king stepped forward and hugged him so tightly that something cracked.

— Dad… my ribs…

— Quiet and accept the love!

Leon burst out laughing.

The king looked at him.
— AND WHO ARE YOU?

— Leon. Crisis companion.

The king nodded.
— Good. The crisis is enormous.

---

They were led to the throne room.

In the middle stood…

a gigantic pancake.

But it was perfectly flat.

Too perfect.

The king sighed dramatically.
— The Golden Pancake has lost its fluffiness.

Leon looked at the dragon.
— That's bad?

The dragon turned pale.
— It's a national disaster.

— I see.

The king began to pace in circles.
— Without fluffiness we cannot begin the Festival of the First Flip!

Leon blinked.
— The Festival of the what?

— The First Flip! When a young dragon tosses a pancake for the first time and doesn't hit himself in the face with it!

Leon looked at his dragon.
— Did you manage it?

The dragon said nothing.

The king cleared his throat.
— He hit the bishop.

Leon burst out laughing.
— WHAT?!

— It was an unfortunate accident! — the dragon protested.

— He had syrup in his nose for a week — the king added.

Leon nearly fell off his chair.

---

— Right — he said at last, wiping tears away. — Why isn't the pancake fluffy?

The king sighed.
— The royal chef has gone missing.

At that moment a dramatic voice rang out:

— BECAUSE I AM HERE!

Everyone turned around.

On the chandelier hung… a chef.

Literally.

Hanging upside down.

— Why are you up there?! — Leon cried.

— I've been stuck since Tuesday!

— Why didn't anyone help?!

The king shrugged.
— We thought it was a modern decoration.

Leon stared blankly into the distance.
— This planet will be the death of me.

---

When they got the chef down, he sighed heavily.

— The secret of fluffiness is simple.

Everyone leaned in closer.

— You must… mix the batter gently.

The king furrowed his brow.
— That's all?

— That's all.

The dragon turned pale.
— I always mix with all my might…

— I KNOW! — the chef shouted. — That's why the batter is like a brick!

Leon looked at the dragon.
— Seriously?

— I thought you had to dominate the pancake.

— It's not a fight!

— How was I supposed to know?!

---

A few minutes later the whole palace was watching as the dragon mixed gently.

Very gently.

So gently that his tongue stuck out with concentration.

Leon was barely holding back laughter.

— You look like a surgeon.

— Quiet.

The batter went into the pan.

Everyone froze.

The pancake rose.

And rose.

And rose still higher.

It was perfectly fluffy.

The king burst into tears.
— This is the most beautiful sight…

The bishop wiped his nose instinctively.
— Reflex from the trauma — he explained.

The Storygenerator blazed:
— *Mission complete: fluffiness restored.*

Everyone began to cheer.

The dragon looked at Leon.
— We did it!

And then…

out of nerves he let out an enormous fart.

**PPRRRRROOOOOOT.**

A wave of air tossed the gigantic pancake into the air.

It flew high.

Very high.

And…

**SPLAT.**

It landed perfectly on the king's head.

Silence fell.

Leon bent double with laughter.

The king removed the pancake from his face and said solemnly:

— Son.

The dragon swallowed.
— Yes, Dad?

The king smiled.

— That was the most perfect First Flip in history.

The whole hall erupted in applause.

The dragon blinked.
— Really?

— Really.

Leon was still laughing.

Because sometimes the greatest successes…

begin with gentle mixing…

and end with a royal pancake on someone's face. 🐉🥞💨👑✨
