The next day something was… off.

Leon felt it right away, before he even got out of bed.

Nothing specific had happened.

It was just that everything felt a little heavier.

Thoughts. Steps. Even the air.

— Great… — he muttered, staring at the ceiling. — This wasn't in the instructions.

He looked at the Storygenerator.

Silence.

Zero help.

— Right… now you're testing me.

At school things were different too.

Nothing bad happened.

But nothing good either.

The boy from the hallway didn't even notice him.

In class Leon knew the answer — and this time… he didn't raise his hand.

He just didn't have the energy.

At break someone was laughing.

Not at him… but he still felt a little on the outside of everything.

— This must be one of those moments — he thought. — The "bad part of the story."

He sat somewhere to the side.

And for the first time in a long while… he didn't want to change anything.

After coming home he dropped his bag a little harder than usual.

— Well? — he said in the direction of the Storygenerator. — Where's all that magic now?

Silence.

Leon sat on the bed and looked at his notebook.

"Stories That Really Happened."

He opened it.

The previous days looked good.

Too good.

He sighed.

— Alright… this is part of it too, isn't it?

He picked up his pen.

He hesitated for a moment.

And then he wrote:

— "Today nothing great happened."

He paused.

— "Today I didn't have courage."

One more moment.

— "But… I'm still here."

He stared at those words for a moment.

They weren't spectacular.

They weren't "heroic."

But they were true.

And then…

click

The Storygenerator lit up.

Leon raised his eyes.

— Seriously? Now?

A message appeared on the screen:

— Hardest level unlocked.

Leon squinted.
— Even harder?!

The screen flickered calmly.

— Ordinary day.

— Goal: don't give up on the story.

Leon snorted quietly.
— Sounds like something I just did… or am trying to do.

A moment of silence.

— Exactly.

Leon leaned against the wall.

— So what… that counts too?

— That counts most of all.

Leon looked at his notebook once more.

At those three sentences.

And for the first time that day he felt something lighter.

Not great joy.

But something sufficient.

— Okay… — he said quietly. — Tomorrow we'll try again.

The Storygenerator didn't answer.

But this time… Leon didn't need an answer.

Because he had understood something important:

Not every part of a story is exciting.

Not every part is easy.

But every part… counts.

Even the one where the only victory is:

"I'm still here." ✨
