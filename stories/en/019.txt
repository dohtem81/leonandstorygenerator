That night aboard "Spark 7" it was unusually quiet.

The dragon slept on a pile of cushions and smacked his lips in his sleep.

— …more syrup… and no raisins…

Leon was sitting by the window when the Storygenerator suddenly glowed with a deep blue light.

Not the red alarm.

Not the golden pancake glow.

Deep blue. Mysterious.

A message appeared on the screen:

**"Adventure mode: NIGHT AT THE MUSEUM OF LOST STORIES."**

Leon smiled broadly.
— Oh, this sounds good.

The dragon opened one eye.
— Are there pancakes?

— I don't know.

— I'll come anyway.

Click.

---

They found themselves in front of an enormous building.

It was beautiful and slightly frightening.

Tall columns, wide steps, hundreds of windows.

Above the entrance a sign read:

**MUSEUM OF STORIES THAT NOBODY FINISHED**

The dragon swallowed.
— This sounds like the kind of place where something will definitely jump out and eat me.

— You're a dragon.

— Exactly. I'm delicious and well-seasoned.

The doors opened by themselves.

*CREEEEAK.*

Leon looked at the dragon.
— That was a bit over-the-top dramatic.

— I really don't like this.

They went inside.

It was dark.

Quiet.

Everywhere stood exhibits covered with white sheets.

The air smelled of old paper and dust.

In the middle of the hall stood a night watchman.

Or rather… his armour.

Empty.

But upright.

— Welcome — it said.

The dragon squeaked in a thin voice:
— AAAA A TALKING OUTFIT!

Leon actually jumped.
— Alright, that got me too.

The armour bowed.
— I am Sir Reginald, the night custodian. We need help.

— What happened? — Leon asked.

Reginald sighed metallically.
— Someone opened the Hall of Unfinished Stories.

The dragon hid behind Leon.
— Sounds bad.

— It's worse. At midnight all the stories will come to life… and begin searching for their endings.

Leon furrowed his brow.
— And if they don't find them?

Reginald looked serious.
— They start making up their own.

Silence fell.

The dragon peeked out.
— …isn't that good?

Reginald shook his helmet.
— Last time a dinosaur became an accountant and pirates opened a hair salon.

Leon blinked.
— Okay, that is actually bad.

---

Suddenly a gong rang out.

**BUMMMMM.**

Reginald turned pale. That is… creaked more nervously.

— Too late.

Throughout the museum, lights blazed on.

And everything… came to life.

A tyrannosaurus skeleton leapt down from its display.

— WHERE ARE MY TAX DOCUMENTS?! — it roared.

Pirates ran down the corridor, shouting:
— WHO WANTS A PERM?!

An Egyptian mummy rode past on a scooter.

A knight was fighting a drinks machine.

The dragon squeaked:
— I want to go home.

Leon watched with delight.
— This is genius.

— This is terrifying!

— A little, yes.

---

The Storygenerator blazed:
— *Mission goal: restore the correct ending to every story.*

Leon clapped his hands.
— Right. We split up.

The dragon looked at him in horror.
— WHAT?!

— Joking.

— Don't do that.

First they caught the tyrannosaurus.

— You're not an accountant! — Leon cried.

— HOW DO YOU KNOW?!

— Because you're a dinosaur!

The tyrannosaurus hesitated.
— …that does make sense.

He immediately returned to his display and roared majestically.

— One.

Then the pirates.

— Hairdressing is not your destiny!

— But we're great at layering! — they protested.

The dragon sighed and said:
— Where is your ship?

The pirates froze.
— …right.

They ran back.

— Two.

The mummy stopped her scooter.

— What about me?

Leon looked at her.
— You probably have a curse and a tomb?

— True. That's more atmospheric.

— Three.

---

It seemed they were winning.

Until they reached the last hall.

The largest.

In the middle stood an enormous empty pedestal.

And above it hung a sign:

**"Exhibit 000: The Story That Was Never Told."**

Leon furrowed his brow.
— What does that mean?

Suddenly something stirred in the darkness.

A figure emerged.

Without a face.

Without a shape.

A shadow woven from blank pages.

— I have no ending — it said quietly. — Because no one ever started me.

The dragon hid behind his own tail.
— Oh no.

Leon stepped forward.

— Maybe… we can start now?

The shadow trembled.
— Is that possible?

Leon smiled gently.

He pulled out his notebook.

He wrote the first sentence:

**"One night in the museum, a story woke up that thought nobody wanted it…"**

The shadow lit up.

The dragon watched in wonder.
— Ooooh.

Leon kept writing.

About a story that was afraid to be told.

About how it found courage.

About how sometimes one sentence is enough.

When he put down the full stop…

the entire museum blazed with golden light.

All the exhibits returned to their places.

Silence returned.

Reginald bowed low.
— You have saved the museum.

The dragon puffed out his chest proudly.
— Yes. Mainly me.

Leon looked at him.
— You spent half the adventure hiding behind a flowerpot.

— Tactically.

---

On the way back to the ship, Leon looked at the Storygenerator.

— That was good.

— *Because every story wants to be told.*

The dragon yawned.
— Even the one about the pancake that became king?

Leon looked at him.
— …what?

The dragon shrugged.
— I have ideas.

Leon sighed.
— I have a feeling tonight is going to be long.

And somewhere in the museum…

on the empty pedestal lay a piece of paper.

On it, one sentence:

**"This is only the beginning."** ✨🏗️🐉📖
