That night aboard "Spark 7" it was unusually quiet.

The dragon slept on a pile of cushions and smacked his lips in his sleep.

— …more syrup… and no raisins…

Leon was sitting by the window when the Storygenerator suddenly glowed with a deep blue light.

Not the red alarm.

Not the golden pancake glow.

Deep blue. Mysterious.

A message appeared on the screen:

**"Adventure mode: NIGHT AT THE MUSEUM OF LOST STORIES."**

Leon smiled broadly.
— Oh, this sounds good.

The dragon opened one eye.
— Are there pancakes?

— I don't know.

— I'll come anyway.

Click.

---

They found themselves in front of an enormous building.

It was beautiful and slightly frightening.

Tall columns, wide steps, hundreds of windows.

Above the entrance a sign read:

**MUSEUM OF STORIES THAT NOBODY FINISHED**

The dragon swallowed.
— This sounds like the kind of place where something will definitely jump out and eat me.

— You're a dragon.

— Exactly. I'm delicious and well-seasoned.

The doors opened by themselves.

*CREEEEAK.*

Leon looked at the dragon.
— That was a bit over-the-top dramatic.

— I really don't like this.

They went inside.

It was dark.

Quiet.

Everywhere stood exhibits covered with white sheets.

The air smelled of old paper and dust.

In the middle of the hall stood a night watchman.

Or rather… his armour.

Empty.

But upright.

— Welcome — it said.

The dragon squeaked in a thin voice:
— AAAA A TALKING OUTFIT!

Leon actually jumped.
— Alright, that got me too.

The armour bowed.
— I am Sir Reginald, the night custodian. We need help.

— What happened? — Leon asked.

Reginald sighed metallically.
— Someone opened the Hall of Unfinished Stories.

The dragon hid behind Leon.
— Sounds bad.

— It's worse. At midnight all the stories will come to life… and begin searching for their endings.

Leon furrowed his brow.
— And if they don't find them?
