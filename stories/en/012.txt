The engines of "Spark 7" hummed a little louder than usual.

Leon smiled broadly.
— Okay, this time I feel it… something big.

The Storygenerator bathed the cockpit in warm light.

— New sector detected: The Zone of Silence.

Leon raised an eyebrow.
— Silence? That sounds suspicious.

— No signals. No stories. No… anything.

— So the perfect place for trouble — he muttered. — Let's go.

The further they flew, the stranger things became.

Stars started disappearing.

Not going out.

Just… not being there.

As if someone had erased a piece of space with a rubber.

— Haven't seen this one before — said Leon, more quietly.

— Warning: entering a region without narrative.

— Without… stories?

— Yes.

The ship slowed on its own.

As if it had nothing to "hold onto."

Leon looked out the window.

Nothing.

Not a black sky.

Not stars.

Just… absence.

— This is a little unsettling — he admitted.

— This place forms when stories stop being told.

Leon furrowed his brow.
— So… someone stopped telling?

— Or someone stopped listening.

A silence fell.

A real one.

Not like before.

This one was… heavy.

Leon tried to say something.

— Alright, so I'll—

His voice… disappeared.

He opened his mouth, but there was no sound.

— Hey?!

He looked at the Storygenerator.

— Voice communication unavailable.

— Great… — he said soundlessly.

The ship stopped completely.

In the middle of "nothing."

And then Leon saw something.

A small point of light.

Far away.

Very far away.

But it was there.

— Okay… that's probably our goal.

He set course.

The ship moved slowly, as if pushing through thick fog.

The closer they got, the brighter the light grew.

Until finally they saw…

a small figure.

Sitting alone.

On something that looked like a fragment of floor suspended in emptiness.

In front of it lay a closed book.

Leon stepped off the ship.

His footsteps felt strange — as if there were no ground, and yet there was.

He stepped closer.

The figure didn't look up.

Leon sat down beside it.

He tried to say something.

Nothing.

He sighed.

He looked at the book.

Slowly he opened it.

Blank pages.

All of them.

He understood.

He reached for the pen in his pocket.

He hesitated for a moment.

And then he wrote:

"There was someone who thought their story didn't matter."

The figure beside him stirred slightly.

Leon kept writing.

"So they stopped telling it."

A small light appeared above the page.

"But that didn't mean the story had disappeared."

The light grew.

The figure raised its head.

"It was simply waiting… for someone to start again."

Suddenly —

sound returned.

As if someone had turned the world back on.

— …and then… — the figure said quietly.

Leon looked at it.

— Exactly — he smiled gently. — And then?

The figure looked at the book.

— And then I tried again.

Light exploded.

The silence broke.

Stars returned one by one, like lamps being lit.

The Storygenerator blazed brightly.

— Zone of Silence resolved.

Leon let out a breath.
— That was… different.

— Sometimes the greatest problem isn't chaos…

Leon finished the sentence:
— …it's the absence of anything.

— Exactly.

The figure disappeared.

But the book remained.

Leon took it with him onto the ship.

On the cover a message appeared:

"Begin, even when it is quiet."

Leon sat in the seat.

He looked at the Storygenerator.

— So where to now?

The screen glowed calmly.

— Wherever someone hasn't started yet.

Leon smiled.

And this time he didn't wait even a second.

— Then we fly.

Because he already knew one thing:

The greatest adventures don't begin with noise.

They begin with the first sentence. 🚀✨
