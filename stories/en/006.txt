Leon closed his notebook and sat in silence for a moment.

It all seemed to be finished.

And yet… something still "hung in the air."

The Storygenerator no longer shone the way it had before. It wasn't calling out, wasn't suggesting adventures.

It stood quietly.

As if waiting.

— Alright — Leon said softly. — So what do we do now?

The screen lit up gently.

But instead of text… a reflection appeared.

Leon's face.

— Okay… a bit odd — he muttered.

And then the image changed.

He saw the boy with the dragon — now sitting at his desk, drawing away. Beside him lay more sheets of paper, filled with increasingly bolder sketches.

The image disappeared.

His younger brother appeared — showing the ship to someone. The crooked one, with two masts. And he was proud of it.

Gone.

Another scene — a complete stranger. A girl in class raising her hand even though she's scared. A boy on the pitch trying again after a missed shot.

Leon furrowed his brow.
— That's… me?

A message appeared on the screen:

— The effect of a story.

— One story never ends in one place.

Leon felt something warm inside. A little like the first time he clicked the button… but quieter.

— So it… spreads further?

— Yes. — replied the Storygenerator. — But…

Leon sighed.
— There's always a "but."

— Not every story goes well.

The screen darkened.

A new scene appeared.

A boy sat alone. The sheet of paper in front of him was blank. A crumpled ball of paper lay nearby.

— I don't want to — he said quietly. — It won't work out anyway…

Leon felt a familiar twinge.

— Oh no…

— This is a story that has stopped again. — the Storygenerator explained.

— So should I go there? Help him?

A short pause.

— You can't be everywhere.

Leon pressed his lips together.
— Then what?

The screen lit up gently.

— You can leave something that will help… even when you're not there.

Leon thought for a moment.

He looked at his notebook.

At his words.

At everything he had been through.

And suddenly he had an idea.

He took a new sheet of paper.

He wrote just one sentence on it:

"It doesn't have to be perfect to be real."

He folded the paper.

— And now what?

The Storygenerator glowed softly.

The piece of paper disappeared.

The screen showed the boy again.

He was sitting just the same.

Nothing had changed…

Until suddenly he noticed something on his desk.

A piece of paper.

He unfolded it.

He read it.

He stopped.

And very slowly… he reached for a pencil.

Leon let out the breath he hadn't even known he was holding.

— It works…

— Stories don't always need a hero present — said the Storygenerator. — Sometimes a trace is enough.

Leon smiled slightly.
— That sounds… important.

The screen flickered one last time that evening.

— New mode unlocked:

— You are the Storygenerator.

Leon looked at the device.

Then at his own hands.

— Well… I think something bigger is starting, isn't it?

The Storygenerator didn't answer.

Because this time… it didn't need to. ✨
