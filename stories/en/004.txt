Leon stared at the Storygenerator as if it were about to dissolve.

— This must be some kind of joke… right?

Silence.

No blinking. No "click me."

Just him. And his room.

— Hello? — He tapped gently on the casing.

Nothing.

And then, for the first time since this adventure had begun, Leon felt something strange… not the fear of a dragon or a shadow, but emptiness. Because what if a story really had to happen… without any help?

He sighed.
— Alright… fine.

He sat on the bed.

A few minutes passed. Nothing happened.

And then… something did happen after all.

He heard a quiet crying.

Not from the Storygenerator.

From the hallway.

Leon got up slowly and opened the door. On the floor sat his younger brother, curled up with his knees pulled to his chest.

— Hey… what happened? — Leon asked.

— I broke the ship… — the boy whispered. — The one I'd been building all week…

Leon looked. On the floor lay pieces of a model. Broken, scattered.

And suddenly something clicked in his head.

This was the story.

There was no magical world. There were no monsters.

There was something harder.

Real life.

Leon sat down beside his brother.
— Wow… that looks serious.

— It can't be fixed anymore — the boy said. — It was pointless to even start.

Leon was quiet for a moment.

He remembered the dragon without wings. He remembered the king without joy. He remembered his own stumbles in the forest.

And suddenly he understood.

— You know what? — he said calmly. — Maybe we can't make exactly the same ship.

His brother looked up at him through tears.
— Exactly…

— But we can make a better one.

— Better?

Leon picked up one of the pieces.
— We'll add something new. Maybe a cannon? Maybe a different colour? Maybe… two masts instead of one?

The boy hesitated for a moment.

— But I don't know how…

Leon smiled gently.
— Neither do I. But we can try together.

A moment of silence.

And then the boy wiped his eyes and nodded.

They began to put the pieces together. Not perfectly. Not the way it was before.

But slowly… something began to take shape.

The ship was a little crooked.

A little odd.

But it had something the previous one hadn't.

A story.

After a while the boy smiled.
— It's even better.

Leon looked at him.
— See?
